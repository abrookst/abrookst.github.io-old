Hello! Welcome to my first blogpost!


Today, I would like to talk about the metaverse. As the weather cools, and fall comes, the "hype" around the metaverse has seemingly waned. I am seeing less and less articles about a metaverse fashion show or how The Oscars made Chadwick Boseman into an NFT. This can be pretty easily seen in the google trends, where "NFT", "Crypto", and "Metaverse" are sitting at an all time low.

google trends for "crypto"


This was honestly inevitable, in more ways than one. Trying to muscle in the new wave of technology with raw acquisitions, money, and hype leads to nothing but a fad. The brighter the star shines, the quicker it burns out. But I do not believe that the metaverse is a fad, and no matter how critical I am of NFTs, I believe it and the blockchain has its place.


As a resident VR dweller, I have seen some of the magic that can come of this virtual world we have crafted. Some of the interactions and experiences I have had in/with XR have been amazing, and really unforgettable. Whether that was the first time I put on an Oculus, back in 2016, and being blown away by the worlds I was taken to, or when I caught my first Pokemon in Pokemon GO, and being amazed at how the pokemon seemed to be right there. We have come a long way, and these technologies have been implemented into our daily lives. I use AR with Google Maps every day, and VR is being used in industries across the world. It is not a fad or some sort of trend, and the immediate next step is to make our little demos and experiences into entire worlds. To take that "world" and turn it into a "universe."


But again, these technologies that have become mainstream were not started in huge multi-billion dollar marketing pushes. They were made by people with a vision and a passion. Sure John Carmack had a hand in Oculus' origin, but Oculus did not have a conference room full of investors waiting for this whole "VR" thing they spent a lot of money investing in. The money came from the community, via Kickstarter. And that's how I believe the metaverse will be made, by those who want to see it done, and it does not have to be done by Q3 2022.


And we almost had it, we almost had that lovingly made community metaverse, with an application called Neos VR, but as ThrillSeeker pointed out in a fantastic video, the hype behind the future of tech is what killed the most advanced VR application we have seen to date.

Neos VR


All I can hope for is that this doesn't become the next Virtual Boy, that the uphill struggle we are already facing to move the metaverse and VR forward wasn't just worsened by a rich visionary at the top of their game once again. To quote Kotaku about Facebook's Metaverse Application "Horizon":


"Horizon is not quite as awful as the ad makes it seem, but it is still a hollow, corporate shell that has more in common with an office than it does a playground, or any other type of social space a human being would willingly want to hang out in. It's definitely no VRchat or Rec Room."


And I wholeheartedly believe that this should not be all the metaverse has to offer. And I hope another project like Neos comes around to show us how the Metaverse should actually be done.
